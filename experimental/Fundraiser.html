Help Jason through his battle with Lymphoma!

Donate Now  Share on Facebook
  • Story
  • Updates  
Created May 9, 2018
Jessica 'jaejae' Julian
  Medical   DELANO, CA

Anyone who has met my best friend Jason knows that he has a heart of gold. But after undergoing a complicated thyroid surgery, Jason was diagnosed with Stage II Lymphoma. He knows that everyday in the near future is going to be a battle with this disease. The doctors say he'll need many treatments of chemotherapy and possibly local radiation as well. This is a rough fight that Jason now finds himself on, but he's ready to wage war through the coming months of chemo treatments, hospital visits, and medications.

He started the fight with chemotherapy the week after his surgery, and he will receive treatments every 21 days for the next 3-4 months. Jason had a few days of nausea and fatigue following the first battle through chemo, but his energy is back up. We’re really hoping that he can recover from this fight and that you can help.

As Jason's best friend, I am raising money to help pay for the many expenses that will come along during the tough battles ahead. He's so strong and wants more than anything to take this whole fight and make it "no big deal." Any little bit helps! We appreciate you all for helping her through this difficult battle! Thank you.

Update 4
Posted by Jessica 'jaejae' Julian
6 months ago
    Share
 
 
Hello family & friends,

Since our last update, Jeffrey completed 7 radiation treatments for his lower back and 17 additional sessions to reduce the lesions/metastasis in his chest.
Roughly about a month after, Jeffrey was feeling better and was able to go about his days and regain his strength--as if this cancer never existed. He reduced the amount of medications and we were so hopeful for his recovery.

Unfortunately, during that time Jeffrey was experiencing headaches. Last Saturday, he was admitted to the ER because his headaches were unbearable. After a CT scan and blood work, results showed that he had swelling and "lesions" in parts of his brain. MRI scans were requested and a doctor determined the lesions were tumors located in different parts of his brain. Jeffrey was in the hospital for five days under tremendously kind care and will need to get further treatments at the oncology center.

Jeffrey wanted us to share his video because many people have asked for an update. He's continued to be in high hopes because of the countless support and love you all have been showing him and our family. Thank you so much for everything!

With love,
The Ganio-Julian Family

Hello everyone,

First and foremost, thank you for taking the time to check in with us and the well-being of Jeffrey. We appreciate everyone for reaching out and the on-going support we've received to meet our goal.

We have a few updates to share--

Over the past couple months, Jeffrey has continued his chemo treatments, medication, and monthly check-ups.
His latest PET scan (pictured) shows signs of lesions/metastasis along his spine, chest, and lower back but we continue to stay hopeful! Jeffrey will need to start radiation treatment once again until further notice.

He completed his first set of chemo treatments and we are thankful to be celebrating his 22nd birthday today! (Happy Birthday, Jeffrey!)

We will take each day as it comes and will do our best to update you when we can. Thank you again and God bless!


With love,
The Ganio-Julian Family
